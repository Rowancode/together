Our Vision

At Together we work for a world which priorities the person above all else. We see the variety and difference between people as the most exciting part of living together. Our job is to create a society in which loving one another is actively promoted such that everyone can become the best version of themselves.

Nothing is more important for this than promoting strong communities made up of caring relationships.

The key causes of poverty: educational failure, worklessness, family breakdown, debt, and addiction all have relationship breakdown at their core. Equally, the increasingly stressful and unsatisfying lives of some of the most successful can be explained in terms of relationship poverty.

Creating a relationship-centred society is the most important issue we face, and holds the key to solving our biggest challenges. The EU Referendum showed that it’s not just individuals who feel isolated. It revealed a huge divide in the nation; an ‘Un-United’ Kingdom.

Together exists to heal these rifts, both in the lives of individuals and the nation as a whole.

What is Together?

At Together we reject the idea that change is only brought about through politics. For us, the most important element to any change begins in the attitudes and values of our culture.

Together consists of THE BLOG and ACT TOGETHER.

The Blog

The Blog exists to influence the discussion.

It is deliberately non-party aligned, prioritising effective problem-solving above political creed. It will provide analysis, commentary on and solutions to the problems which have the greatest effect on people’s lives.

Each article will stress the merits of a relationship-centred approach and in doing so hopes to move the debate to focus on the areas which have most relevance to our everyday lives.

Act Together

Act Together seeks to go beyond words.

Here we begin to work towards the kind of change we’ve identified in THE BLOG. We will partner with charities, social enterprises and community action groups to campaign for change and support grassroots movements on the ground.
